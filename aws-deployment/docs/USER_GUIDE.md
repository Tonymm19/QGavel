## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 Logging In

**Step 1:** Open your web browser and go to:
```
https://qgavel.com
```

**Step 2:** Enter your credentials:
- **Email:** Your registered email address
- **Password:** Your password (provided by your administrator)

**Step 3:** Click **"Sign In"**

> üí° **Tip:** Use "Remember Me" on trusted devices to stay logged in.

### 1.2 First-Time Login

When you log in for the first time:
1. You'll be prompted to change your temporary password
2. Choose a strong password (at least 8 characters, mix of letters and numbers)
3. You'll be taken to your Dashboard

### 1.3 Forgot Password?

1. Click **"Forgot Password?"** on the login page
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your email for a reset link
4. Follow the link to create a new password

## 3. Judges & Procedures

QGavel contains detailed information about federal judges and their specific procedures.

### 3.1 Viewing the Judge List

1. Click **"Judges"** in the sidebar
2. You'll see a list of all judges in the system
3. Currently includes **8 N.D. Illinois (Northern District of Illinois) judges**

### 3.2 Judge Profile Information

Click on any judge's name to see their profile, which includes:

| Section | Information |
|---------|-------------|
| **Basic Info** | Name, courtroom, phone, email |
| **Chamber Staff** | Law clerks, judicial assistants |
| **Procedures** | Judge-specific filing requirements |
| **Court Rules** | Links to relevant local rules |

### 3.3 Understanding Judge Procedures

Each judge may have specific requirements for:
- **Motion filing** - Page limits, formatting requirements
- **Discovery disputes** - Pre-motion conference requirements
- **Trial procedures** - Exhibit handling, witness lists
- **Scheduling** - Preferred hearing times, availability

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important:** Always check judge-specific procedures before filing. They can differ significantly from general court rules.

### 3.4 Searching for Judges

Use the search bar at the top of the Judges page:
- Search by **name** (e.g., "Pallmeyer")
- Search by **courtroom number**
- Filter by **district** (when more districts are added)

## 4. Managing Cases

### 4.1 Creating a New Case

**Step 1:** Click **"+ New Case"** button (or go to Cases ‚Üí New Case)

**Step 2:** Fill in the required information:

| Field | Description | Required? |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Case Number** | Court case number (e.g., 1:24-cv-01234) | Yes |
| **Caption** | Case name (e.g., Smith v. Jones) | Yes |
| **Court** | Select the court | Yes |
| **Judge** | Assigned judge (if known) | No |
| **Practice Area** | Type of case (civil, criminal, etc.) | No |
| **Filing Date** | Date case was filed | No |
| **Status** | Open, Closed, Pending | Yes |

**Step 3:** Click **"Create Case"**

### 4.2 Viewing Your Cases

1. Click **"Cases"** in the sidebar
2. See all your cases in a list
3. Use filters to sort by:
   - Status (Open, Closed, All)
   - Judge
   - Practice Area
   - Date range

### 4.3 Case Details Page

Click on any case to see:
- **Case Information** - All details you entered
- **Assigned Judge** - With link to judge profile
- **Deadlines** - All deadlines for this case
- **Activity History** - Changes made to the case

### 4.4 Editing a Case

1. Open the case
2. Click **"Edit"** button
3. Make your changes
4. Click **"Save"**

### 4.5 Assigning a Judge to a Case

1. Open the case
2. Click **"Edit"** or find the Judge field
3. Select the judge from the dropdown
4. Click **"Save"**

> üí° **Tip:** When you assign a judge, QGavel automatically shows that judge's procedures for easy reference.

## 5. Deadline Management

This is the core feature of QGavel - never miss a deadline again!

### 5.1 Creating a Deadline

**Step 1:** Navigate to a case, then click **"+ Add Deadline"**

**Step 2:** Fill in the deadline information:

| Field | Description | Required? |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Title** | Brief description (e.g., "Response to Motion to Dismiss") | Yes |
| **Due Date** | When it's due | Yes |
| **Due Time** | Time of day (defaults to court closing time) | No |
| **Type** | Filing, Hearing, Conference, Other | Yes |
| **Priority** | High, Medium, Low | No |
| **Notes** | Additional details | No |
| **Reminder** | When to be reminded | No |

**Step 3:** Click **"Create Deadline"**

### 5.2 Viewing Deadlines

**From the Dashboard:**
- See upcoming deadlines for the next 7 days

**From the Deadlines Page:**
1. Click **"Deadlines"** in the sidebar
2. See all deadlines across all cases
3. Filter by:
   - Date range
   - Case
   - Priority
   - Status (Pending, Completed)

**From a Case:**
- Open any case to see deadlines specific to that case

### 5.3 Deadline Status

Deadlines have visual indicators:

| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| üî¥ **Red** | Overdue or due today |
| üü° **Yellow** | Due within 3 days |
| üü¢ **Green** | Due in 4+ days |
| ‚ö™ **Gray** | Completed |

### 5.4 Completing a Deadline

When you've met a deadline:
1. Find the deadline
2. Click the **checkbox** or **"Mark Complete"**
3. Optionally add a completion note

### 5.5 Editing a Deadline

1. Click on the deadline
2. Click **"Edit"**
3. Make your changes
4. Click **"Save"**

### 5.6 Deleting a Deadline

1. Click on the deadline
2. Click **"Delete"**
3. Confirm the deletion

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Warning:** Deleted deadlines cannot be recovered.

## 6. Calendar Integration

Export your deadlines to your favorite calendar application.

### 6.1 Exporting to Calendar

**Step 1:** Go to **Deadlines** or a specific **Case**

**Step 2:** Click **"Export to Calendar"** or the calendar icon

**Step 3:** Choose export options:
- **All Deadlines** - Export everything
- **This Case Only** - Just deadlines for current case
- **Date Range** - Specific time period

**Step 4:** Download the `.ics` file

### 6.2 Importing to Your Calendar

**For Microsoft Outlook:**
1. Double-click the downloaded `.ics` file
2. Outlook will open and show the events
3. Click **"Import"** or **"Add to Calendar"**

**For Google Calendar:**
1. Go to Google Calendar
2. Click the gear icon ‚Üí **Settings**
3. Click **Import & Export** ‚Üí **Import**
4. Select the `.ics` file
5. Choose which calendar to add to
6. Click **"Import"**

**For Apple Calendar:**
1. Double-click the `.ics` file
2. Choose which calendar to add to
3. Click **"OK"**

### 6.3 Calendar Tips

- Export regularly to keep your calendar updated
- Set up a recurring reminder to re-export weekly
- Consider creating a dedicated "QGavel Deadlines" calendar

## 7. Tips & Best Practices

### 7.1 Daily Workflow

**Morning routine:**
1. Log into QGavel
2. Check Dashboard for today's deadlines

4. Plan your day around critical deadlines

1. Create the case in QGavel immediately

4. Add all known deadlines right away
